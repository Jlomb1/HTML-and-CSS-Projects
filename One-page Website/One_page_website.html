The Axolotl

What is this animal, and why is it so important?

The Axolotl is a species that comes from Mexico, specifically Lake Xochimilco and Lake Chalco, both lakes
are no longer around. These small creatures are a mix between a lizard and a fish and have an extremely
important role in understanding cell-regeneration.
These little guys are unfortunately on the brink of extinction due to human city expansion. These animals
serve an important purpose in understanding stem-cell research. This is because an axolotl is one of the
one of few species to be able to regenerate almost any part of its body, even some parts of the brain!
Another fact about these creatures is that they retain juvenile characteristics into adulthood, meaning
they essentially stay in a "child like" state even as they get older
